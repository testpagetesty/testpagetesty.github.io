Basic Rules

Objective of the Game

The objective of poker is to win the pot (bet) by forming the best five-card hand or making other players fold.

Hand Rankings

  1. Royal Flush
  2. Straight Flush
  3. Four of a Kind
  4. Full House
  5. Flush
  6. Straight
  7. Three of a Kind
  8. Two Pair
  9. One Pair
  10. High Card

Game Structure

  • Pre-flop: Initial card distribution
  • Flop: First three community cards
  • Turn: Fourth community card
  • River: Fifth and final community card
  • Showdown: Revealing cards

Basic Strategies

Position at the Table

  • Early Position: More caution
  • Middle Position: Greater flexibility
  • Late Position: More aggression
  • Button: Most advantageous position

Hand Selection

  • Premium: AA, KK, QQ, AK
  • Strong: JJ, TT, AQ, AJ
  • Speculative: Low pairs, suited connectors
  • Marginal: Avoid in early position

Basic Concepts

  • Pot Odds: Ratio of bet to pot
  • Implied Odds: Potential gains
  • Equity: Probability of winning
  • Range: Possible hand combinations

Advanced Tips

Bankroll Management

  • Do not play more than 5% of bankroll
  • Maintain detailed records
  • Establish stop-loss
  • Gradually increase limits

Reading Opponents

  • Observe betting patterns
  • Identify common tells
  • Analyze decision timing
  • Keep notes on players

Mental Control

  • Avoid tilt
  • Maintain concentration
  • Accept variance
  • Play within comfortable limits
